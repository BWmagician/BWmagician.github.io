Before starting Gaussian elimination, we should figure out a question first. What does multiplying a matrix on the left side mean?

Let $A$ be $m$ by $n$ and $B$ be $n$ by $p$, $A_i$ represent the $i$ th row of $A$, and $B_i$ represent the $i$ th column of $B$

In the discussion above we explain the idea of multiplying a matrix on the right,

The idea of multiplying a matrix on the left is,

Giving a close look on $A\times B_i$ (here $B_i$ refers to column), it forms the $i$ th column of $C$, which means each column of $C$ is a linear combination of $A$’s column.

Giving a close look on $A_i\times B$, (here $A_i$ refers to row), it forms the $i$ th row of $C$, which means each row of $C$ is a linear combination of $B$’s row.
